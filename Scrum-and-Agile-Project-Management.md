## Setting up Jira


![Jira Home](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/atlassian_home_1-0.png)
1. Create an [Atlassian](https://www.atlassian.com/) account.


![New Jira workspace](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/jira_home_2.png)
2. Open up a new Jira workspace.

![Jira options](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/jira_options_3.png)
3. Choose the Scrum template.

![Scrum Template](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/scrum_choose_4.png)
4. Click on "Use Template".

![Scrum Template 2](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/scrum_options_5.png)
5. Click on "Select a team-managed space".

![Space settings](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/scrum_setup_6.png)
6. Give the workspace a name, and let it generate a key.

![Jira invite](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/invite_7.png)
7. Invite your teammates to the workspace (each member must create an Atlassian account).

![Jira done](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/scrum_done_7.png)
8. You have setup your Scrum workspace!

## Setting up the Gantt Chart

![Select timeline](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_select_13-0.png)
1. Select Timeline on the navigation bar.

![Add epic](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_13.png)
2. Add epics to your Gantt chart. These are longer term goals that you want to set at the beginning of the semester, and if there are more objectives to be done, you could also add new ones along the way.

![Gantt items](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_settings_14-1.png)
3. You can change the dates of the epics by clicking on the timeline and dragging the bars, or click on the epics to change the details of the objectives.

![Gantt details](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_details_15.png)
4. In the detailed view, you can change the color (you can color-code different goals), asignee, start and end dates, etc. You can also add child work items, which would be sub-tasks of the objective.

![Gantt details](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_child_17-2.png)
5. Adding child work items would automatically populate them as tasks in your product backlog.

![Subtask work type](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/gantt_work_type_18.png)
6. Clicking into the child work items, you can change their work types. You would likely be using the "Task" work type, but you can change them to whichever one you see fit.

## Product and Sprint Backlog

![Select backlog](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/backlog_select_8-0.png)
1. Select Backlog on the navigation bar.

![Create in backlog](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/backlog_8.png)
2. If you have made child work items in the Gantt chart, you would see them in the backlog. If not, you can create tasks in directly in the product backlog (this would not be reflected on the Gantt chart).

![Added backlog items](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/backlog_items_8.png)
3. You can change the task status and assign them to your teammates.


![Backlog details](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/backlog_items_8.png)
4. You can also change other task settings by clicking on the task and opening up the detailed view.

![Backlog details](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/backlog_item_details_9.png)
5. You can also change other task settings by clicking on the task and opening up the detailed view.

![Sprint log](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/sprint_log_10.png)
6. Drag the tasks from the backlog to the sprint log above. After adding the tasks, click Start Sprint. More tasks could be added after the sprint has begun.

![Start sprint](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/start_sprint_11.png)
7. Set the start and end date for the sprint, and click Start.

![Sprint board view](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/sprint_boad_12.png)
8. After starting the sprint, you will be taken to the board view. You can go back to the Backlog from the navigation bar to add tasks or change task status. Remember to constantly update your sprint log and backlog to reflect your progress. When the sprint is completed, click Complete sprint (you can also do this is the Backlog view).

![Complete sprint](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/sprint_complete_16.png)
9. If you have tasks that are not marked as "Done" when completing a sprint, you can choose to have them be moved to a new sprint or to the backlog.

## Sprint Burndown Chart

![Add report](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/reports_18.png)
1. The sprint burndown charts are available in the Reports view. On the navigation bar, click on the Add button, then click on Reports, and lastly, click on Add to navigation.

![Report home](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/reports_home_19.png)
2. In the Reports view, you can look at different stats of your project. For the sprint burndown chart, click on More reports.


![Burndown](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/reports_more_20.png)
3. Choose the Sprint burndown chart.

![Sprint burndown](images/Scrum_and_Agile_Project_Management/burndown_21.png)
4. Choose the correct sprint for your burndown chart, and select Work item count for the Estimation field.
